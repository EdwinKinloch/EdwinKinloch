About

I'm Edwin Kinloch — a platform and infrastructure engineer based in the southeastern United States. I've spent the last fifteen years on the unglamorous side of software: the servers, the pipelines, the 2 a.m. cutovers, the compliance findings nobody wants to own. Lately that's meant hardening AWS environments to federal security standards during the day, and building self-hosted AI agent systems on my own time.

My background is a mix that doesn't show up on a lot of resumes: I started racking servers and imaging laptops, worked my way through Active Directory and VMware, and eventually landed in cloud-native Kubernetes and Terraform work. I like systems that have to actually survive contact with production — the AI stuff included. If it can't handle a bad day, it isn't done.

Outside of work I'm usually on the mats training Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu, behind on a manga backlog, or out in the garden pretending I know what I'm doing. More on all of that under Hobbies on the blog.
